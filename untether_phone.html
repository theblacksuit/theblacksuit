WORK LIFE BALANCE. Untether from work calls, emails. Practicing self care. Stop phone addiction.

In the previous video, I received requests to discuss how to balance life and work.

With the holiday season coming up, it's especially important to manage your work life to that you can still enjoy the holidays.

Some folks have asked that I share my personal experience with work/life balance.

  • My first year, I was not very good. I worked almost all day on Thanksgiving.
  • The next few years were also not great, I worked a lot.
  • This year, I've taken more personal time, as it has been a difficult year with the pandemic.

Also, it's important to give youself time to enjoy the holidays and not feel tethered to work.

Research has shown that the more we feel tethered to out phone and/or work while at home, the more likely we are to feel tense and unhappy (source).

Other side effects of poor work life balance include:

  • Psychological distress
  • Family feeling like you're not present
  • Feeling stressed and upset. One Thanksgiving I was so preoccupied with work that I felt too busy to preheat the oven to cook the turkey!

Here are some tips that can help!

  1. Tell yourself that you will not check your phone
    1. If this isn't possible, check your phone only twice per day.
    2. Say it out loud to yourself. Get a mirror!
    3. Or - write your commitment down on a piece of paper!
  2. Put your phone in a different room or hide it so that you don't impulsively check it
    1. Have a family member hide it for you (you won't know where it is)!
    2. Make a bet with someone that you won't use your phone.
  3. If you want to start with baby steps first, TURN OFF NOTIFICATIONS
    1. Airplane mode
    2. Do not disturb mode
    3. Deactive alerts for specific apps
  4. Do some other activities to distract yourself from the urge to check your phone
    1. Cook turkey. Try a new mashed potatoe recipe.
    2. Host video chats or conferences with your friends and family.
  5. Remember that rarely will an issue or task be so urgent that you have to respond immediately
    1. Courts are closed on holidays. Clients are not always working.
    2. You can respond on Black Friday or on Saturday or Sunday.

If you try these tips, you'll feel more relaxed and able to enjoy the holiday

If someone were to ask me today if I regret working on holidays, I would say that I don't, but I'm not sure it made much of a difference in the long run.

  • Would have been fine whether I had completed a project on Thanksgiving, or a bit later.
  • If you don't untether, you're more likely to burn out and make bad decisions.
  • Some firms have recognized this and have policies in place to prevent employees from checking emails or logging in to work systems after hours.

So remember, untether from your phone and work! It is healthy and will make you more productive in the long run.
